# Knowledge Graph Theory

## What is a Knowledge Graph?

A knowledge graph represents information as a network of entities (nodes) and their relationships (edges). Unlike traditional databases, knowledge graphs capture the semantic meaning of connections.

## Key Concepts

### Nodes and Edges
- **Nodes** — Entities, concepts, or pieces of information
- **Edges** — Typed relationships between nodes
- **Properties** — Metadata attached to nodes or edges

### Graph Metrics
- **Degree Centrality** — Number of connections a node has
- **Betweenness Centrality** — How often a node lies on shortest paths
- **Community Detection** — Identifying clusters of related nodes

## Why It Works for Personal Knowledge

The human brain doesn't store information in folders — it builds associations. A knowledge graph mirrors this natural structure:

- **Bidirectional links** let you traverse ideas in any direction
- **Emergent structure** — patterns emerge from connections, not predefined hierarchies
- **Serendipitous discovery** — you find connections you didn't know existed

## Applications

- Personal knowledge management (PKM)
- Research and literature review
- Project documentation
- Learning and spaced repetition
