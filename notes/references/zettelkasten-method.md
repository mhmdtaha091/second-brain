# Zettelkasten Method

The Zettelkasten (German for "slip box") is a note-taking method developed by Niklas Luhmann, one of the most prolific social scientists of the 20th century.

## Core Principles

### Atomicity
Each note contains exactly one idea. One concept, one insight, one fact. This makes each note a reusable building block.

### Connectivity
Every note must link to at least one other note. Links create context, structure, and serendipity. A note without links is a note you'll never find again.

### Emergence
Don't impose hierarchy upfront. Let structure emerge from the connections between atomic notes. Clusters form naturally around themes.

### Iterative Refinement
Notes are living documents. Update them as your understanding deepens. Add new links, revise conclusions, split or merge as needed.

## Types of Notes

1. **Fleeting Notes** — Quick captures, processed within 24 hours
2. **Literature Notes** — What you read, in your own words
3. **Permanent Notes** — Synthesized ideas, your original thinking
4. **Structure Notes** — Maps of topics, like tables of contents
