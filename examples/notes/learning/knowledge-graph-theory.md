# Knowledge Graph Theory

A knowledge graph represents information as a network of entities (nodes) and
relationships (edges). Unlike folder hierarchies, it captures the semantic
meaning of connections.

## Key Concepts

- **Degree centrality** — how many connections a node has
- **Betweenness centrality** — how often a node lies on shortest paths
- **Community detection** — finding clusters of related nodes

## Why It Works for Personal Knowledge

The brain doesn't store information in folders — it builds associations:

- **Bidirectional links** let you traverse ideas in any direction
- **Emergent structure** — patterns emerge from connections, not hierarchies
- **Serendipitous discovery** — you find connections you didn't know existed
